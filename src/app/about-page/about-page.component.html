About

Enzymes serve as the molecular machines of life and essential biocatalysts for numerous biotechnological and biomedical applications. Open Enzyme Database (OED) is a community-driven, open-access repository and infrastructure for sharing enzyme data. The database serves as a critical resource for researchers and practitioners in biochemistry, molecular biology, enzymology, protein and metabolic engineering, and related fields, currently offering quantities of curated data on enzyme kinetics parameters with experimental support. The development of the maximum common substructure (MCS) algorithm, along with chemical similarity and fragment algorithms, has also been completed. These tools will be instrumental in identifying potential enzymes capable of catalyzing new substrates.
Looking ahead, OED plans to expand its scope to encompass a wide array of enzyme-related information, including data on enzyme solubility, stability, specificity, selectivity, functions and associated enzymatic reactions, thereby becoming a comprehensive resource for the scientific community.
With a focus on providing access to extensive enzyme data, OED is committed to serving as an indispensable resource not only for experimental scientists in diverse fields like drug discovery, metabolic engineering, synthetic biology, and synthetic chemistry, but also for machine learning experts who are interested in enzyme property and function prediction and enzyme engineering.

Statistics

The current release of Open Enzyme Database includes quantities of dataset, featuring 87,833 entries for enzyme turnover number (kcat), 169,264 entries for Michaelis constants (Km), and 51,698 entries for enzyme catalytic efficiency (kcat / Km).

Huimin Zhao
Project Director
Dr. Huimin Zhao (https://chbe.illinois.edu/people/profile/zhao5) is the Steven L. Miller Chair Professor at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). He is currently the director of NSF AI Research Institute for Molecule Synthesis (https://moleculemaker.org), the director of NSF iBioFoundry, and the director of NSF Global Center for Reliable and Scalable Biofoundries. He received his Ph.D. degree in Chemistry from the California Institute of Technology in 1998 under the guidance of Nobel Laureate Dr. Frances Arnold. Dr. Zhao has authored and co-authored over 453 research articles and over 30 issued and pending patent applications with several being licensed by industry. Dr. Zhao is the Editor-in-Chief of ACS Synthetic Biology and the Founding Associate Editor of ACS Catalysis (2011-2022). His primary research interests are in the development and applications of synthetic biology, machine learning, and laboratory automation tools to address society’s most daunting challenges in health, energy, and sustainability.
