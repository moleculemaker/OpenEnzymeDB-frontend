About

Enzymes serve as the molecular machines of life and essential biocatalysts for numerous biotechnological and biomedical applications. Open Enzyme Database (OED) is a community-driven, open-access repository and infrastructure for sharing enzyme data. The database serves as a critical resource for researchers and practitioners in biochemistry, molecular biology, enzymology, protein and metabolic engineering, and related fields, currently offering quantities of The development of the maximum common substructure (MCS) algorithm, along with chemical similarity and fragment algorithms, has also been completed. These tools will be instrumental in identifying potential enzymes capable of catalyzing new substrates.
Looking ahead, OED plans to expand its scope to encompass a wide array of enzyme-related information, including data on enzyme solubility, stability, specificity, structures, functions and associated enzymatic reactions, thereby becoming a comprehensive resource for the scientific community.
With a focus on providing access to extensive enzyme data, Open Enzyme database is committed to serving as an indispensable resource not only for experimental scientists in diverse fields like drug discovery, metabolic engineering, synthetic biology, and synthetic chemistry, but also for machine learning experts who are interested in enzyme property and function prediction and enzyme engineering.

Statistics

The current release of Open Enzyme Database includes quantities of dataset, featuring 87,833 entries for enzyme turnover number (kcat), 169,264 entries for Michaelis constants (Km), and 51,698 entries for enzyme catalytic efficiency (kcat/Km).

Team

Meet the team behind Open Enzyme Database.
Huiming Zhao
Project Director
Dr. Huimin Zhao (https://chbe.illinois.edu/people/profile/zhao5) is the Steven L. Miller Chair Professor and Director of NSF AI Research Institute for Molecule Synthesis (https://moleculemaker.org) at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). He received his Ph.D. degree in Chemistry from the California Institute of Technology in 1998 under the guidance of Nobel Laureate Dr. Frances Arnold. Dr. Zhao has authored and co-authored over 340 research articles and over 30 issued and pending patent applications with several being licensed by industry. His primary research interests are in the development and applications of synthetic biology, machine learning, and laboratory automation tools to address society’s most daunting challenges in health, energy, and sustainability.
Le Yuan
Postdoc UIUC
Dr. Le Yuan is a postdoctoral researcher at the Carl R. Woese Institute of Genomic Biology (IGB) at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, under the supervision of Prof. Huimin Zhao. Prior to this, he completed his Ph.D. degree in the Department of Life Sciences at Chalmers University of Technology. Dr. Yuan has published 16 academic papers in prestigious journals, including Nature Catalysis, Nature Protocols, Nature Communications, etc. His primary research interests include synthetic biology, systems biology and machine learning.
